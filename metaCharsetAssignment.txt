<meta>: This is an HTML meta tag used to provide metadata about the HTML document.
charset="UTF-8": This attribute specifies the character encoding of the document. 
In general, <meta charset="UTF-8"> ensures that the browser knows how to correctly interpret and display characters in the document, 
including special characters and characters from different languages(Mandarin, Arabic, or Japanese etc). 


Significance of meta charset utf-8.
------------------------------------------
The significance of using the meta charset UTF-8 in HTML documents lies in ensuring proper handling and display of characters, especially non-English characters and special symbols, across different browsers and platforms. Here are some key points about its significance:

Unicode Support: character encoding capable of representing all possible characters in the Unicode standard. Unicode covers almost all characters, symbols, and scripts used worldwide, making UTF-8 a versatile encoding method.

Internationalization: With UTF-8, you can include characters from various languages and writing systems, ensuring your content is accessible to a global audience. 

Special Symbols: UTF-8 supports a wide range of special symbols, mathematical symbols, currency symbols, and emojis. 

Consistency Across Platforms: Specifying the charset as UTF-8 helps maintain consistency in how characters are interpreted and displayed across different browsers, operating systems, and devices.  

SEO and Accessibility: Using UTF-8 ensures that search engines can properly index and understand the content of your website, including non-English text.
